About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at the Operations Research Center at MIT advised by Professor Robert M. Freund and Professor Bart Van Parys.

My research is primarily in optimization—both theory and application—and often both simultaneously. I have been developing methodologies that automate the construction of provably fastest algorithms for classes of optimization problems, including many of those arising in machine learning, business analytics, and data science more broadly. These methodologies formulate algorithm design and analysis as solvable optimization problems in and of themselves and have led to several significant and impactful applications, such as discovering optimal algorithms and constructing novel mathematical proofs. Very recently, my methodologies helped pave the way for novel accelerated gradient descent methods by other researchers (see [Link 1], [Link 2], [media coverage]). In addition to my research in theory and computational tools in optimization, I have been working on developing sustainable transportation systems. My research in this area has been implemented in the largest installed base of communication-based train control systems worldwide and has been deployed in major cities around the world, including London, Hong Kong, Shanghai, New York, and Vancouver.

I obtained my M.A.Sc. from the ECE Department, University of Toronto in 2016. After that, I worked as a researcher at the Research & Technology Department of Thales Group for almost three years. Previously, I obtained my B.Sc. in Electrical and Electronic Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology.
